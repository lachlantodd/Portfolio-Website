Bachelor of Engineering (Honours)

Mechanical Major
James Cook University, Townsville

I decided to study a Bachelor of Mechanical Engineering to challenge myself. I chose this pathway after challenging myself in school by studying the "suicide six" of subjects, the heaviest in maths and science. I am a logical thinker with a very mathematically and scientifically driven brain. I enjoy solving problems which require a good amount of understanding and active thinking.

My minor was in Intelligent Systems. I had always enjoyed and maintained programming as a hobby and through this minor I learnt how to control embedded systems. This gave me exposure to programming Freedom boards with C++ in Kinetis Design Studio, as well as creating my own printed circuit boards (PCBs) and using VHDL. My favourite assignment was where we had to design PCBs to interface with a 3D printed crane, and control it through its servos using our own C++ code and joystick components.

Shortly after completion of my undergraduate course, I discovered the Master of Information Technology (Software Development) at QUT. I saw this as a way to steer my pathway in the direction of my long-term passion and hobby of computer programming with my underlying desire to solve challenging and meaningful problems.
